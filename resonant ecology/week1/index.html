I am a huge fan of Cosmo Sheldrake. I have been listening to his music for years, recognising the sounds of his non-human collaborators - from the birds to the fish to sounds of the water and earth. I didn’t think much about his ideologies and perspective of the nature world he indulged in his project, until I listened to his talk at the Biotopia Sense Festival about his album ‘Wake up call’.

Monica Gagliano, an ecologist known for her research on plant intelligence, believes we should give voices to other species, to the planet itself. ‘Resonant Earth' was a project that used science to support these voices, to question your knowledge and learn to listen to the non-humans, giving them a position to help guide and teach us about the world we presume to be anthropocentric.

In his talk, Cosmo believed the same. He sampled songs of endangered birds but didn’t claim all the work to himself. 50% profit of the album will be sent to charities working with that ecosystem restoration around his non-human collaborators. Cosmo wanted to learn how to recognize more than human entities in a legal framework to attribute royalties to nature, making collaborative music go beyond human way. He was giving them their voices, recognising their contributions in a human-centric world.

Animals have a lot of interesting ways to make sounds. They sing and communicate like we all do and to give them more credit is to understand what the land needs. I listened to Cosmo’s music soon after that, realising how humans can collaborate with animals, for our music and voice can be heard together.
